Committees

Over the course of three days of Pacific Model United Nations 2017, delegates will spend a large portion of their time in committee. They will debate issues, think of solutions, and forge alliances, in the variety of committee setting that PacificMUN will be offering this year. The choice of committee will allow delegates to customize to their strengths and interests, but regardless of committee, we can guarantee an unforgettable experience for all delegates who wish to learn about world issues, represent country policy, and practice the diplomacy that is mandated by the United Nations.

With committee choice in mind, PacificMUN will be offering fifteen diverse committees that will allow for a fit to every delegates' preference. Be it debating global issues in a GA such as SPECPOL, controversial political debate of the US Senate, or the fast paced problem solving of the Joint Crisis Committee, PacificMUN has something to offer for everyone. Through all of these committees, we aim to give delegates the opportunity to represent nations in all sorts of settings, as in the real world, there is no telling what issue the United Nations may face next.

This year at PacificMUN, we have chosen to orient several topics around controversy and divisiveness, as we feel that not all diplomatic solutions are achieved by everyone simply agreeing. We offer a challenge to delegates, as passionate debate and innovative diplomacy will be necessary to find comprehensive solutions, such as the real United Nations. We hope that through topics that will split policy apart, we hope to bring delegates together to discuss real world issues with a more realistic approach, all while learning about global events and geopolitics.

We look forward to delegates receiving their assignments and beginning their research, and awaiting your arrival in February!
