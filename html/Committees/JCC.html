JCC

JCC: The year is 2018. Quebec has seceded from Canada, becoming their own independent nation. A Cold War has erupted between these two nations, with the far-right Canadian Nationalist Front gaining power over the Canadian Parliament. With the backing of France and the European Union, La Republique Démocratique Du Québec (RDQ) has flourished, depressing the Canadian economy.

After a new uranium deposit found in Quebec and military treaties signed between the RDQ and France, tensions are at an all-time high. Border skirmishes between the two countries’ militaries, Canada and Quebec, and announcements of support by their respective allies, the United States of America and France, bring these two nations on the brink of war.

Questions, inquiries, and position papers about this committee can be emailed to jcc@pacificmun.org
